Features

With all post types and every Tumblr feature supported, Riptide is a theme for everyone!

Responsive Design

Riptide is adapted to suit (almost) every possible device out there. Your blog will look equally beautiful, wherever you may be - at home, or at sea.

Multi-Column

Choose the maximum amount of columns for your blog posts in theme options, and they will automatically adapt as the browser window gets smaller.

Riptide uses the popular Masonry plugin to line your posts up in a perfectly tiled grid.

Theme Options

Customize your theme in your own liking with over 100 different Theme Options!

Retina Ready

With help of the wonderful Font Awesome project, all of the icons used in Riptide are retina ready!

Not only that. Photos and portraits are also optimized to look nice and crisp on your retina devices!

Smart Sidebar

With the custom made sidebar, only for Riptide, your columns will beautifully adapt to the new content width as the sidebar opens. This means you can still browse through your posts, even when the sidebar is opened.

Social Feeds

Display posts from other social media websites on your blog with the Social Feeds! Available for Twitter, Instagram, Dribbble and Flickr.

If you need help to set it up, take a look at the tutorials below.

Social Icons

Riptide ships with support for 40 social icons seen below. All you need is a link to paste into Theme Options, and the icon will automatically be added to the sidebar!

Oh, and are you missing an icon? Don't worry. Send me a message and I'll be happy to help you set it up!

Disqus Comments

Riptide supports commenting systems by Disqus on Permalink pages. There's also a Theme Option that allows you to enable Disqus for Custom pages.

Permalink Pagination

Browse your blog content easily with Permalink pagination. On Permalink pages, pagination arrows will be displayed in the header, waiting to take you to the next post!

Other Features

Group blogs, Day Pages, Tags and Tag pages, Localization, Search box, Like and Reblog buttons, Lightbox, High-res photos, Infinite Scroll, Google Analytics, Tool tips & more!

Support

Riptide supports all post types.

Text, Photo, Photoset, Panorama, Quote, Link, Chat, Audio, Video, Ask, Submit

Browser Support

Chrome, Opera, Safari, Firefox, IE9+

Theme Installation

To install Riptide, start by logging in to your Tumblr Dashboard. Locate and click on the Settings icon in the top menu. Scroll down until you find the 'Edit theme' button, next to Website Theme.

You can also reach the customize page with this link: "https://www.tumblr.com/customize/YourBlogName".Make sure to replace YourBlogName with your actual blog name.

Click 'Edit HTML' right below Custom theme.

Now it's time to grab your downloaded files from Theme Forest. Open the file named 'Riptide.html' in a text editor. Copy everything (CMD+A or CTRL+A to select everything, and CMD+C or CTRL+C to copy it) and paste it (CMD+V or CTRL+V) into the opened sidebar.
Make sure to remove any already existing HTML code before you replace it with your new theme code.

You're almost done! Now simply click Update Preview then Save, and Riptide is installed!

There's just a few more steps to make sure Riptide works properly.

Go back to where you found the 'Edit HTML' link, scroll down to the very bottom of the page. You should see a button saying 'Advanced options'.

After you've clicked the button, you'll see a few options. Make sure 'Use default mobile theme' is turned OFF, otherwise Riptide will not work on mobile browsers.

Next, you should change the 'Posts per page' option. If Infinite Scroll is enabled, I recommend setting it to '15'. If not, choose whatever you like!

Congratulations! You have successfully set up your new theme. Now you just have to configure the theme options, located on the customize page, to make Riptide look the way you want.

And as always, if you're having any problems with the theme, I promise I'll do my best to help you.

Setting up Social Feeds

Social Feeds can be tricky to set up on your own, but here are some tutorials to guide you on the way.
If you still are having problems, you know what to do!

Twitter

There's already a great tutorial by the Tumblr Team themselves on how to connect your blog to Twitter, so there's no need for me to explain.

Connecting your Tumblr to Twitter

Instagram

To set up your Instagram Feed, you need two things. A User ID, and an Access Token. Head to jQinstapics, click the 'Retrieve my details bro!' button, authorize with Instagram (don't worry, you can trust this website), and your details will magically appear!

Dribbble

Luckily, not all feeds are tricky to set up. Simply put your Dribbble Username in the theme option, and you're good to go!

Flickr

Setting up your Flickr feed is almost as easy as with Dribbble. All you need is your Flickr Username. Head to idGettr, follow the easy instruction, and there you go!
